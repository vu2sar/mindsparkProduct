Skill In need of Relative Improvement - Reading Comprehension.

The users seem to have scored relatively lower on Reading. This implies that they need to work towards improving reading-oriented skills, such as recall, inference and extrapolation. In order to do this, one must primarily develop a habit of reading regularly and for an extended period of time. To encourage reading habits, here are a few activities which you could try out.
  • You could form a teachers’ book club and conduct monthly discussions and presentations. Have ‘best presenter’ prizes to incentivize participation.
  • You could ask teachers to give book recommendations for the library, and randomly allot those recommendations to different teachers. Ask them to mutually discuss.
  • English teachers can conduct monthly sessions (and annual workshops) on effective comprehension exercises. You could also use the study material of higher grades for a casual worksheet exercise to keep teachers’ refreshed and aware of the need to develop English language skills.
 However, skills are always interdependent, and therefore the best way to acquire a language is through continued exposure to listening, speaking, reading and writing.

Key points from Essay evaluations –
Most of the teachers faced issues with basic grammar techniques. The most common errors made were –
  1. Punctuation - incorrect/negligible use of commas and capitalizations.
  2. Sentence structures - run on sentences, incorrect positioning of words in a sentence [normally because of translating from their mother tongue]
  3. Tenses [using present tense for general statements], subject- verb agreement [singular/plural - using ‘uniforms is’ instead of ‘uniforms are’]
  4. Vocabulary - using words without understanding the meaning in context – e.g. “uniforms make a person unique”.
  5. Structure - Ideas lacked clarity and structure, were not supported with explanations or examples - points were merely listed. The introduction – explanation - conclusion format was not followed in any essay.
  6. Lots of problems with the use of articles and prepositions (where to use 'the', where to use 'on' vs. 'in')
  7. Misuse of conjunctions (‘and’, ‘but’, ‘or’, ‘since’) while joining parts of sentences.
  8. Several spelling errors such as ‘pshycological’ instead of ‘psychological’ and ‘wear’ instead of ‘where’.
